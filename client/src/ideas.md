<idea>
  **Design Movement**: **Brutalist Sci-Fi / Cyber-Industrial**
  
  **Core Principles**:
  1.  **Raw Functionality**: UI elements should look like heavy machinery controls, unpolished metal, and terminal interfaces.
  2.  **High Contrast & Monospace**: Prioritize legibility and data density over "pretty" gradients.
  3.  **Immersive Diegesis**: The UI *is* the ship's computer. No floating modals that feel "gamey"; everything is a panel or a readout.
  4.  **Glitch & Analog Decay**: Subtle CRT effects, signal noise, and "boot up" sequences to ground the tech in a gritty reality.

  **Color Philosophy**:
  -   **Void Black (#050505)**: The background is the emptiness of space.
  -   **Terminal Amber (#FFB000)** or **Phosphor Green (#00FF41)**: The primary active color, representing old, reliable tech.
  -   **Alert Red (#FF3333)**: For combat and critical warnings.
  -   **Steel Grey (#2A2A2A)**: For structural containers.
  -   *Intent*: To evoke the feeling of operating a powerful but aging starship in a hostile universe.

  **Layout Paradigm**:
  -   **Bento Box / Modular Grid**: The screen is divided into strict, resizable rectangular panels (like a tiling window manager).
  -   **Peripheral Navigation**: Main controls are anchored to the edges (bottom command bar, top status rail), leaving the center for the tactical map or main viewport.

  **Signature Elements**:
  1.  **Scanlines & Vignette**: A permanent, subtle overlay to simulate a monitor.
  2.  **Technical Borders**: 1px borders with corner ticks and technical labels (e.g., "SYS.01", "NAV-DAT").
  3.  **Data Visualizers**: Bar graphs and histograms that constantly update with "noise" even when idle.

  **Interaction Philosophy**:
  -   **Tactile Feedback**: Buttons should feel "heavy" (instant down state, delayed up state). Sounds are mechanical clicks and hums.
  -   **Instant Data**: Hovering over *anything* gives a detailed technical readout (tooltip) instantly.

  **Animation**:
  -   **Snap & Flicker**: Transitions shouldn't be smooth fades. They should snap into place or flicker on like a fluorescent light.
  -   **Typewriter Effect**: Text streams in character by character.

  **Typography System**:
  -   **Headers**: *JetBrains Mono* or *Space Mono* (Uppercase, tracked out).
  -   **Body**: *Share Tech Mono* or *Fira Code*.
  -   *Rule*: 100% Monospace. No variable width fonts allowed.
</idea>

<idea>
  **Design Movement**: **Ethereal Imperial / Art Deco Futurism**
  
  **Core Principles**:
  1.  **Opulence & Order**: The UI reflects the grandeur of an interstellar empire. It is elegant, symmetrical, and imposing.
  2.  **Golden Ratio**: Layouts strictly adhere to divine proportions.
  3.  **Celestial Light**: Glows are soft, diffuse, and radiant, like starlight through a nebula.
  4.  **Ceremonial Interface**: Actions feel like decrees. Buttons are ornate seals.

  **Color Philosophy**:
  -   **Deep Royal Purple (#1A0B2E)**: The canvas of the empire.
  -   **Gold Leaf (#D4AF37)**: For borders, active states, and important data.
  -   **Starlight White (#F8F9FA)**: For primary text, glowing softly.
  -   **Nebula Teal (#008080)**: For scientific or navigational data.
  -   *Intent*: To make the player feel like an Emperor or High Admiral, detached from the grime, commanding from a throne.

  **Layout Paradigm**:
  -   **Central Axis**: Important elements are centered and symmetrical.
  -   **Floating Glass**: Panels are semi-transparent, frosted glass floating above a slow-moving starfield background.

  **Signature Elements**:
  1.  **Geometric Patterns**: Art Deco sunbursts or intricate filigree as background textures.
  2.  **Glowing Gradients**: Borders that fade from gold to transparent.
  3.  **Serif Typography**: A rare choice for Sci-Fi, adding a sense of history and tradition.

  **Interaction Philosophy**:
  -   **Fluidity**: Interactions are slow, smooth, and deliberate.
  -   **Ritualistic**: Confirming an action triggers a satisfying, magical flare effect.

  **Animation**:
  -   **Slow Fade & Drift**: Elements drift gently into place.
  -   **Shimmer**: Gold borders occasionally shimmer as if catching light.

  **Typography System**:
  -   **Headers**: *Cinzel* or *Playfair Display* (Elegant, high-contrast serif).
  -   **Body**: *Lato* or *Montserrat* (Clean geometric sans-serif for readability).
</idea>

<idea>
  **Design Movement**: **Holographic Tactical / Glassmorphism 2.0**
  
  **Core Principles**:
  1.  **Augmented Reality**: The UI feels like a HUD projected onto the world.
  2.  **Depth & Layers**: Heavy use of blur, transparency, and Z-axis layering to show hierarchy.
  3.  **Clean & Clinical**: No grime, no decoration. Pure information efficiency.
  4.  **Contextual Adaptive**: The UI changes shape and color based on the current alert level (Peace, Caution, Combat).

  **Color Philosophy**:
  -   **Glassy Blue (#E0F7FA with 10% opacity)**: The base material for panels.
  -   **Hologram Cyan (#00E5FF)**: The primary accent and text color.
  -   **Warning Orange (#FF9100)**: For alerts.
  -   **Void Dark Blue (#0A1929)**: Background, deep and cool.
  -   *Intent*: To evoke a high-tech, advanced military simulation.

  **Layout Paradigm**:
  -   **HUD Overlay**: Elements are arranged in a curve around the player's focal point, as if on a curved visor.
  -   **Collapsible Complexity**: Panels can be minimized into small icons to clear the view.

  **Signature Elements**:
  1.  **Frosted Glass (Backdrop Filter)**: Everything blurs what's behind it.
  2.  **Thin Hairlines**: 0.5px borders that look like laser projections.
  3.  **Hexagonal Motifs**: Used sparingly for grids and map markers.

  **Interaction Philosophy**:
  -   **Responsive Light**: Buttons glow brighter when hovered, illuminating nearby elements.
  -   **Swipe & Gesture**: Interactions feel like manipulating a touch surface.

  **Animation**:
  -   **Unfold & Expand**: Panels unfold from a central point.
  -   **Pulse**: Critical elements pulse gently.

  **Typography System**:
  -   **Headers**: *Rajdhani* or *Orbitron* (Squared-off, technical sans).
  -   **Body**: *Inter* or *Roboto* (Neutral, highly readable).
</idea>
